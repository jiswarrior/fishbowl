🎯 Bug Catching Techniques

  • Sneak approach: Hold the "walk" key (default Ctrl) to move silently. Bugs are less likely to flee.
  • Smoke Candles: Craftable at the campfire (requires 1 plant fiber + 1 sweet leaf). When placed on the ground, it creates a smoke cloud that prevents bugs from running away within its radius. Essential for rare bugs.
  • Bait: Certain bugs (like Vampire Moth) can be lured by placing glow worms on the ground. Wait a few seconds for them to appear.
  • Net upgrade: Higher tier nets (Iron, Palium) have a larger capture radius and faster swing speed, making it easier to catch fast bugs.

Kilima Valley

  • Flower Fields (south): Common Butterfly, Blue Butterfly.
  • Forest (west): Firefly (night), Spitfire Cicada (afternoon).
  • Home Garden (after rain): Garden Snail.

Bahari Bay

  • Highlands (east): Rainbow Butterfly (daytime).
  • Cliffs (north): Moonstruck Moth (night).
  • Desert Ruins (southwest): Golden Scarab (summer noon).
  • Caves (any): Glowbug (night), Vampire Moth (fall night).

🐞 Bugs Required for Bundles/Quests

  • Rainbow Butterfly – Temple of Gales bundle (rare).
  • Golden Scarab – “Golden Fortune” quest from Zeki.
  • Firefly – “Light the Way” quest (requires 5 fireflies).
  • Glowbug – Used to craft Glow Worms (fishing bait).

Keep at least one of each rare bug in storage for future bundles or weekly challenges.

💡 Pro Catching Tips

  • Use smoke candles liberally – They are cheap to craft and prevent bugs from fleeing.
  • Run diagonally – Bugs’ movement AI is weaker at angles.
  • Collect bugs during rain – Snails and some rare bugs appear only in rainy weather.
  • Nighttime is best for rares – Many valuable bugs spawn only after 8 PM.
  • Upgrade net to Palium asap – The larger capture radius lets you catch bugs from farther away.
